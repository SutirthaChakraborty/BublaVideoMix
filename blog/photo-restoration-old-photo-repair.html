Types of Photo Damage We Can Repair

Fading & Discolouration

Old colour photos fade to washed-out, yellowish or pinkish hues over time. Black-and-white photos develop brown or sepia tones and lose contrast. We restore original tonal balance, bring back colour accuracy and enhance contrast to return photographs to their original appearance.

Physical Tears & Creases

Photos torn in half, cut with scissors, or heavily creased can be digitally reconstructed. Missing sections can often be intelligently filled using the surrounding context. Deep fold lines and crease shadows are removed cleanly.

Water & Flood Damage

Water-damaged photos develop tide marks, blotches and areas of complete loss. Our restoration team has successfully recovered photographs flooded during the frequent monsoon floods that affect Jalpaiguri district — restoring them as gifts to surprised and grateful families.

Mould & Fungus Damage

Photos stored in damp conditions often develop mould spots that eat into the emulsion. We carefully scan and digitally remove mould damage, then reprint the restored image.

Scratch & Dust Marks

Negatives and prints accumulate scratches over decades of handling. We remove these marks invisibly, preserving the integrity of the original image.

Colourisation of Black & White Photos

A popular service — we can add natural, realistic colour to your old black-and-white family photographs. Imagine seeing your grandparents in vivid colour for the first time. This service makes a deeply meaningful gift for family gatherings and commemorative occasions.

💡 Tip: Don't throw away damaged photos — even severely deteriorated photographs can often be partially or fully restored. Bring it to us and we'll give you an honest assessment before any work begins.

Our Restoration Process

  1. High-resolution scanning — We scan your original photograph at 600–1200 DPI to capture every detail
  2. Assessment & quote — Our editor assesses the damage and provides an accurate time and cost estimate
  3. Digital restoration — Layer by layer, damage is removed and missing areas reconstructed
  4. Review & approval — You see the restored digital file before we print
  5. Premium reprinting — Final restored image printed on archival photo paper for a further 100+ year lifespan
  6. Digital file provided — You receive the high-resolution digital file to share with family
