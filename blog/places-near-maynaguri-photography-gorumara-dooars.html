Places Near Maynaguri — Gorumara, Jalpesh Temple & the Dooars

By Photo Square · Maynaguri, Jalpaiguri · Updated March 2026

Maynaguri — also written Mainaguri — is one of the most strategically located towns in Jalpaiguri district, West Bengal. Situated roughly 10 km north-east of Jalpaiguri city and just 50 km from Siliguri, it sits at the very entrance to the famous Dooars region. Within a short drive you can reach ancient temples, dense national parks, scenic river viewpoints and peaceful picnic forests. And when you return with a camera full of stunning shots, Photo Square at Maynaguri Bus Stand is right here to print, laminate and frame every memory.

🛕 Famous Temples Near Maynaguri

Jalpesh Temple (Jalpesh Mandir) — 3 km from Maynaguri

The Jalpesh Mandir is a celebrated 350-year-old Shiva temple located just 3 km from Maynaguri town. It is one of the most important religious sites in North Bengal, known for its unique blend of architectural styles and its spiritual atmosphere. Thousands of pilgrims descend during Shivratri and the Shravan mela. The temple complex offers beautiful photo opportunities — the ornate gateway, the sacred pond and the surrounding landscape are all highly photogenic.

Jatileswar Temple — 10 km from Maynaguri

Another ancient Shiva temple, Jatileswar Temple dates back to the Gupta period (320–600 AD). Located about 10 km from Maynaguri near the Jaldhaka River, it is one of the oldest archaeological sites in the district.

Bateshwar Temple

A small historic temple on the banks of the Jaldhaka river, popular among local devotees and history enthusiasts.

Mahakal Temple (Mahakal Cave)

A local sacred site known as the Mahakal Cave or Mahakal Temple, visited by villagers and pilgrims from surrounding areas. Related to the Baba Mahakal traditions of North Bengal.

🌳 Gorumara National Park & Wildlife

Gorumara National Park (Lataguri) — 15–20 km from Maynaguri

Gorumara National Park (⭐ 4.4 / 5, 7,600+ Google reviews) is one of the most popular wildlife destinations in West Bengal. Situated in the Dooars belt just 15–20 km from Maynaguri, Gorumara is home to:

  • One-horned Indian rhinoceros
  • Wild Asian elephants
  • Indian gaur (bison)
  • Leopards and barking deer
  • Over 200 species of birds

The park entrance is at Lataguri, making Maynaguri the ideal base for Gorumara safari trips. Photographers love the morning and evening safari slots for wildlife shots in natural light. After your safari, Photo Square can print your best wildlife captures in large formats — 12×18, 20×24 or even 24×36 inches — perfect for framing and display.

Chapramari Wildlife Sanctuary

Chapramari Wildlife Sanctuary (⭐ 4.6 / 5) adjoins Gorumara and is known for its watchtowers offering panoramic views over elephant herds and dense forest. Excellent for bird photography and elephant watching. A favourite for photographers and nature lovers visiting the Dooars.

🌊 Rivers, Viewpoints & Scenic Spots

Jaldhaka River View Point — 14 km from Maynaguri

The Jaldhaka River flows through the Dooars with pristine, clear water. The view point about 14 km from Maynaguri is a serene photography spot — the river, forest backdrop and Himalayan foothills create a stunning natural canvas.

Kuhuli View Point & Kuhuli Bridge

Located near Maynaguri, the Kuhuli Bridge and adjoining view point (⭐ 4.4 / 5, 426+ ratings on JustDial) is a popular photography and sunset spot. The Kuhuli stream reflects the Dooars landscape beautifully.

Gajoldoba (Teesta Barrage) — 20 km

Gajoldoba is where the massive Teesta Barrage creates a wide, lake-like expanse of water. It is a world-class birdwatching site, especially during winter when migratory birds arrive. The sunrise here, with mountains reflected in the calm water, is among the most photographed scenes in North Bengal.

Domahoni Teesta View Point

A scenic confluence point where the Teesta River forms beautiful braided channels. Popular for picnics and riverside photography.

Murti River View Point

A picturesque spot by the Murti River, popular with tourists seeking a quiet, photogenic riverside experience.

🌲 Forests, Picnic Spots & Adventure

Khattimari Forest — 22 km from Maynaguri

Khattimari Forest is a dense natural forest about 22 km from Maynaguri. It is part of the wider Dooars forest belt and offers nature walks, bird spotting and a tranquil forest atmosphere.

Balka Danga Picnic Spot

A popular riverside picnic spot near Maynaguri, frequented by families from Jalpaiguri and Siliguri. The Balka Danga Lohar Bridge is also a noteworthy landmark near here.

Jamaldaha Picnic Spot

Another well-loved local picnic ground near Maynaguri, ideal for winter outings and family gatherings.

Jayanta Ox Bow View Point

A beautiful meander of the river visible from this viewpoint, creating a perfect horseshoe-shaped ox bow lake — a stunning natural photography subject.

Maynaguri Park (Town Park)

Within Maynaguri town itself, the Maynaguri Park is a green local park with playgrounds and open lawns — ideal for family photos and casual outings.

🗺️ Best Time to Visit Maynaguri's Attractions

  • Jalpesh Temple / Jalpesh Mandir: Monsoon (July–August) for Shravan mela; winter for photography
  • Gorumara National Park: November to March (dry season, best wildlife sightings)
  • Gajoldoba Teesta Barrage: October to February (migratory birds, calm water)
  • Rivers and picnic spots: November to February (winter season, pleasant weather)
  • Dooars tea gardens: March–April (spring flush — bright green tea leaves)
